The Massacre in the Great Temple

The Massacre in the Great Temple marked a major shift in Aztec-Spanish relations, which had, up until then, been largely friendly. Panfilo de Narvaez had been ordered to come to the Aztec Empire and arrest Hernan Cortes, forcing Cortes to leave to fight him. While the Aztecs celebrated Toxcatl, an Aztec festivity, after receiving permission from Pedro de Alvarado, the deputy governor in place of Cortes, the Spanish soon entered the Great Temple and massacred almost all in attendance. The few who escaped over the walls described the Spanish atrocity to the community. The Massacre at the Great Temple represented a breakdown in Spanish-Aztec relations.
