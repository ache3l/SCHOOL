FEATURES

After Hernan Cortes landed in the Aztec Empire, he soon met with Moctezuma II, initiating Spanish-Aztec relations. The Aztecs were both happy to welcome Hernan Cortes, but also suspicious of what he could be up to, leading to lukewarm Aztec-Spanish relations. Many priests and other influential figures were there to welcome Cortes, suggesting that Moctezuma II wanted to make friendly contact with Cortes. However, Cortes describes barricades and stoned cities, which shows the Aztecs were preparing for war, potentially against Cortes. This shows us that the Aztecs had a mistrust of Hernan Cortes, and planned to turn against him. In the end, Cortes would attack the Aztec Empire first, showing that the Spanish had equal doubt in Moctezuma.
